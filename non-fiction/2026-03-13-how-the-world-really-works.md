# How the world really works

Vaclav Smil

Link: <https://www.amazon.co.uk/How-World-Really-Works-Scientists/dp/0241454395>

This book essentially argues that the transition to renewable energy will be
_hard_ because of just how embedded fossil fuels are. Besides being used for
heating and transportation, they are also used for food production,
specifically to produce fertilizer.

Overall the book was interesting to read, mostly due to its presentation of
long historical trends of things like energy production and food production.
That being said, I've been exposed to similar ideas before, so nothing in the
book was really shocking or moving. I'd give the book 6/10.
