# Breakneck: China's Quest to Engineer the Future 

Dan Wang

Link: <https://www.amazon.co.uk/Breakneck-Chinas-Quest-Engineer-Future/dp/0241729173>

This isn't just a book about China- it's a comparison of China and America,
giving praise and criticism to both countries. The core of the critique is:

- China and America are overall very similar (economically, but also
  culturally, e.g. driven by work).
- China is an "engineering state": a state run by engineers that sees things as
  engineers do.
- America is a "lawyerly state": a state run by lawyers that sees things as
  lawyers do.
- This difference in focus produces both good and bad things about each
  country:
    - China is great at building things (like infrastructure). America is great
      at making rules that _prevent_ anything from being built.
    - America is better at safeguarding people by having rules. China often
      tries to "engineer" its people and finds that this doesn't work, or
      doesn't work out correctly (e.g. the one child policy)
    - China built a good manufacturing base while America let its manufacturing
      slip away
    - China handled the beginning of COVID well (enforcing lockdowns), unlike
      America, but handled the second part (vaccines and going back to normal)
      a lot worse.

Overall it was a good book, mostly for its anecdotes. I listened on audiobook
and quite liked it!
